About

Muslim heritage stands as a testament to centuries of innovation, spanning from advancements in science and architecture to profound literary and philosophical works, all underpinned by a rich tapestry of cultural exchange and governance rooted in Islamic principles.

Collection Highlights


Damascus Room

Explore a residential reception chamber (qaʿa) typical of the late Ottoman period in Damascus, Syria. On view at The Met Fifth Avenue in gallery 461. The Damascus Room is a stunning example of traditional Islamic architecture and interior design. It is a meticulously decorated reception room that originated from Damascus, Syria, during the Ottoman period.


Sultan ‘Ali ‘Adil Shah II Slays a Tiger

This painting is one of only a few known portraits of Sultan ‘Ali ‘Adil Shah II of Bijapur, India (r. 1656–72). It depicts the ruler engaged in a demonstration of his hunting prowess-- iconography meant to illustrate his role as a strong leader. In reality the sultan was met with continual infiltrations by the rival Mughals and Marathas, and eventually left the affairs of the state to his minister.

Mosque-lamp

Mosque-lamp, made of stonepaste painted in blue, green and black under a transparent glaze; the lamp has a pyriform body with three applied ear-shaped handles and a moulding at the juncture with the trumpet neck, on a spreading profiled foot.
